What is a Car Installment?

A car installment is a method of buying a car where you pay for it gradually over time instead of paying the full price upfront. This is usually done through a loan or financing plan from a bank, financial institution, or dealership.

  • Down Payment: An initial amount paid upfront (usually 10–30% of the car price).
  • Monthly Installments: Smaller payments made every month over an agreed period (e.g., 12, 24, 36 months).
  • Interest: Extra money paid to the lender as a fee for borrowing the money.

Benefits of Car Installments

  • Makes expensive cars more affordable.
  • Allows you to buy a car without saving the full price upfront.
  • Can help build credit history if payments are made on time.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Total payment is usually higher than the car’s cash price because of interest.
  • Late payments can incur penalties and affect your credit score.
  • Some financing plans may require a guarantor or proof of income.
