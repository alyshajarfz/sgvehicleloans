Why Renew COE?

When a car reaches 10 years, owners must choose to renew COE or scrap the car and buy new (often costly). COE renewal is more economical than buying new/used cars, with lower depreciation and reliable performance beyond 10 years.

What is COE & PQP?

COE: Grants vehicle ownership & road usage for 10 years.
PQP: Amount paid to renew COE for 5 or 10 years based on average COE prices over last 3 months. Varies monthly across categories A, B, C, D & E. Latest PQP figures published by LTA after each bidding exercise.

Important Considerations

  • No need to bid for a new COE — just pay PQP
  • 100% loan coverage, interest rates from 2.78%
  • PARF rebate forfeited once COE is renewed
  • Must renew COE before expiry; apply 4–6 weeks before
  • Renewal allowed up to 1 month after expiry, late fee applies

Should I renew COE for 5 or 10 years?

Car owners can choose a 5-year or 10-year COE renewal based on how long they plan to drive the vehicle, its condition, and value.

A 5-year renewal requires only 50% of the PQP, but it can only be done once. At the end of 5 years, the vehicle must be deregistered, except for commercial vehicles eligible for a second 5-year renewal.
